Spellcasting

Each spell has a specific effect and requires certain conditions to be met in order to use. The following is a glossary of terms to get you ready.

- Spell Level -

Each spell has a level. The level represents what level Spell Slot (will be defined below) must be spent to cast it. Ex: A 3rd level spell like Major Image requires a 3rd level spell slot to cast.

You can spend a higher level spell slot (3 for example) to cast a lower level spell (1st level Healing Word for example). When you do it sometimes has a benefit like adding more dice, and it sometimes provides no benefit.

The higher level the spell, the more powerful it is.

- Cantrips -

These are â€œlevel 0â€ spells, meaning that they can be used as many times as you want in a day without needing spell slots.

- Spell Slots -

The main resource that you spend to cast a spell (excluding cantrips). You have a number of different leveled spell slots per day. For example, as a level 5 bard, your character sheet shows Steve has:

  • Four 1st level spell slots
  • three 2nd level spell slots
  • two 3rd level spell slots

- Spell Characteristics -

Each spell has requirements and details on how the spell behaves. A spell usually has a specific effect, but if you are creative, you can use spells in unpredicted ways. The following are characteristics of a spell.

  • Spell Attack or Spell Save

    • Usually a spell that affects an enemy creature requires either a spell attack roll from you or a spell saving throw.

    • Spell Attack - You roll a d20 and add your Spell Attack Modifier to see if it beats the enemyâ€™s defenses.

    • Spell Saving Throw - Instead of you rolling to attack, the enemy rolls to defend against your spell. They must roll the same or above your Spell Save DC to defend. The type of saving throw is listed under the â€œSAVEâ€ column if a save is required.

    • You can find both your Spell Attack Modifier and Spell Save DC at the top of your Spellcasting page.

  • Casting Time
    • Each turn in combat, you get 1 Action, 1 Bonus Action, and 1 Reaction. Spells need either an Action, Bonus Action, or Reaction to cast.
    • One caveat is that if you cast a 1st level spell or higher with your Bonus Action, you cannot cast another leveled spell as an Action. In essence, 1 leveled spell per turn. You can still Attack with a weapon or perform another action though.
  • Range
    • How far away the target can be for the spell to take effect
  • Duration
    • How long the spell lasts
  • Concentration
    • If under Duration you see, â€œConc.â€, that means the spell requires extra attention. You can only have one concentration spell active at a time. You can still cast other spells and do other actions, but if you cast another concentration spell, the first ends.
    • If you are attacked while concentrating, you will have to roll a save to maintain concentration. If you fail, the spell is lost.
